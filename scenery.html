The Breathtaking Scenery of Korea

Beyond its bustling cities and rich history, South Korea is a land of stunning natural beauty. From dramatic mountain ranges and serene national parks to picturesque coastlines and tranquil islands, the country offers a diverse array of landscapes that change dramatically with each season. Discover the serene temples nestled in verdant forests, the vibrant fall foliage, snow-capped peaks, and the calming rhythm of the sea.

Hallasan National Park, Jeju Island

Dominating the center of Jeju Island, Hallasan is a dormant volcano and South Korea's highest peak. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for its unique volcanic landscape, diverse flora, and beautiful hiking trails. The crater lake at its summit, Baengnokdam, is a spectacular sight, especially after a fresh snowfall. The park's trails vary in difficulty, offering options for casual strolls and challenging climbs, all while providing breathtaking views of the island and the surrounding sea.

Seoraksan National Park

Located in the northeastern part of South Korea, Seoraksan is one of the country's most famous and picturesque mountain ranges. Known for its jagged peaks, deep valleys, and crystal-clear streams, it's a paradise for hikers and nature lovers. The park is especially beautiful in autumn when the foliage turns into a riot of reds, yellows, and oranges. Popular spots include Ulsanbawi Rock, a dramatic granite formation, and the serene Sinheungsa Temple, guarded by a massive bronze Buddha statue.

Boseong Green Tea Fields

While not a traditional "scenery" in the mountain or ocean sense, the Boseong Green Tea Fields offer an incredibly unique and beautiful landscape. Rolling hills covered in meticulously manicured rows of vibrant green tea bushes create a stunning, serene vista. Visitors can walk through the fields, enjoy fresh green tea and green tea-infused treats, and soak in the peaceful atmosphere. It's a truly picturesque and calming experience, especially during sunrise or sunset.
